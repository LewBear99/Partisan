Nikola Tesla

Back to Inventors

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor and electrical engineer best known for his work in developing alternating current (AC) electrical systems and wireless communication. His innovative ideas and contributions laid the foundation for modern electricity distribution and wireless technology.

Key Contributions

  • Alternating Current (AC): Developed AC power systems, which revolutionized electricity distribution.
  • Tesla Coil: Created a resonant transformer circuit used in radios, televisions, and wireless energy.
  • Wireless Energy Transmission: Pioneered the concept of wireless power transfer, envisioning a global wireless energy network.
  • Radio Waves: Demonstrated radio transmission in 1893, predating Marconi's developments in wireless telegraphy.
