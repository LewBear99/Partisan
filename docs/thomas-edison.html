Thomas Edison

Back to Inventors

Thomas Edison was an American inventor and businessman, credited with developing the electric light bulb, phonograph, and motion pictures. His work in electricity and innovation greatly influenced the modern industrialized world.

Key Contributions

  • Light Bulb: Invented the first practical incandescent light bulb, revolutionizing indoor lighting.
  • Phonograph: Developed the phonograph, the first device to record and reproduce sound.
  • Motion Pictures: Pioneered the development of motion picture technology.
  • Electrical Power Distribution: Established the first electric utility, providing reliable electric power to homes and businesses.
