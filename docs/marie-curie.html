Marie Curie

Back to Inventors

Marie Curie was a Polish-born physicist and chemist who conducted pioneering research on radioactivity. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and remains the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different scientific fields.

Key Contributions

  • Radioactivity: Coined the term and conducted groundbreaking research on radioactive elements.
  • Radium and Polonium: Discovered the elements radium and polonium.
  • Nobel Prizes: Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics (1903) and the Nobel Prize in Chemistry (1911).
  • Medical Applications: Pioneered the use of radioactivity in medicine, particularly in cancer treatment.
