Albert Einstein was a theoretical physicist renowned for developing the theory of relativity, one of the two pillars of modern physics. His groundbreaking contributions have reshaped our understanding of space, time, and gravity.

Key Contributions

  • Theory of Relativity: Revolutionized the understanding of time, space, and gravity.
  • Photoelectric Effect: Provided crucial evidence for the particle nature of light, earning him the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921.
  • Mass-Energy Equivalence: Introduced the famous equation E=mcÂ², highlighting the relationship between mass and energy.
  • Unified Field Theory: Sought to unify the fundamental forces of physics into a single theoretical framework.
