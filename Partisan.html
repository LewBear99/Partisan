Partisan

Nikola Tesla

Known for his contributions to alternating current (AC) electricity, wireless energy, and more.

Thomas Edison

Famous for inventing the phonograph, electric light bulb, and early motion picture technology.

Marie Curie

Pioneering work in radioactivity and the first woman to win a Nobel Prize.

Albert Einstein

Developed the theory of relativity and made significant contributions to theoretical physics.

Ada Lovelace

Considered the first computer programmer for her work on Charles Babbage's early mechanical general-purpose computer.

Alexander Graham Bell

Inventor of the telephone and contributed to the fields of communication and speech.

Leonardo da Vinci

Renaissance polymath known for his contributions to art, science, and engineering.

Galileo Galilei

Played a major role in the scientific revolution, known for his work in physics, astronomy, and scientific methodology.

Hedy Lamarr

Co-invented frequency-hopping spread spectrum, which is the basis for modern wireless communication.

Tim Berners-Lee

Inventor of the World Wide Web and played a significant role in its development.

Katherine Johnson

Mathematician whose calculations of orbital mechanics were critical to the success of early U.S. spaceflights.
